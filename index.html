Royal Enfield

History and Heritage
Royal Enfield originated in 1901 in Redditch, England, initially manufacturing bicycles before transitioning to motorcycles. The brand supplied military motorcycles during World War I, solidifying its legacy within the British motorcycling industry2. In 1955, Royal Enfield partnered with Madras Motors in India, marking its significant transition to Indian ownership. Today, Royal Enfield operates as a subsidiary of Eicher Motors Limited, an Indian automaker1.
Iconic Models
Royal Enfield is known for its classic-looking motorcycles, including the Royal Enfield Bullet, Classic 350, Thunderbird, Meteor 350, Classic 500, Interceptor 650, Continental GT, and Himalayan. These bikes are equipped with single-cylinder and twin-cylinder engines, catering to various market segments1.
Recent Developments
Recently, Royal Enfield has been working on expanding its lineup with new models like the Classic 650, which is based on the same platform as the Super Meteor 650 and the Shotgun 650. The company is also venturing into the electric motorcycle market with the Flying Flea EV, featuring cutting-edge technology and smart tech integration.
Market Presence
Royal Enfield has a strong presence in India and is expanding globally. The brand's motorcycles are known for their durability, classic design, and the iconic "thump" of their engines, which has become synonymous with Indian motorcycling culture5.
